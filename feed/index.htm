Durian season in Malaysia: A feast for Durian lovers

The durian season is a time of cultural celebration, with peak months from March to December bringing an abundance of durians and lower prices. The beginning and end of the year generally see a reduced supply, and October typically has no durians available.

FAQ

What is the difference between a Malaysian Durian and a Thailand Durian?

Malaysian durians, like Musang King, are known for their creamy texture and blend of sweet and bitter flavours. They have deep yellow flesh.

Thai durians, such as Monthong, are milder in flavour, less creamy, and have pale yellow or white flesh. They are larger and sweeter, but less complex in taste.

What is the size of a Musang King durian core?

The core of a Musang King durian varies in size, but it’s generally large due to the fruit’s thick flesh and small seeds. Each lobe of the durian can be several inches long and wide.

How to know if a durian is ripe?

  • Smell: Ripe durians have a strong, distinctive aroma.
  • Sound: Shaking a ripe durian reveals slight movement of the seed inside.
  • Touch: The shell should be slightly soft under pressure.
  • Appearance: Look for a natural opening or crack at the bottom of the durian.
